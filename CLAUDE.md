# Portfolio Copy Style Guide

## Voice
- Direct, honest, grounded. No grand declarations.
- Write like a confident professional, not a personal brand.
- Avoid pretentious or self-aggrandizing statements (e.g. "I AM design", "I find structure where others find noise" type of phrasing).
- Credentials and experience should feel earned, not performed.

## Punctuation
- No em dashes (—). Use a period or rewrite the sentence.
- No semicolons. Split into two sentences instead.
- Prefer short sentences over long compound ones.

## Words and phrases to avoid
- "I am X" as an identity claim (e.g. "Design is how I am")
- "never" as an absolute (e.g. "I never stop until it's right" is ok, "I have never been just a designer" is not)
- Filler transitions: "Additionally", "Furthermore", "It is worth noting"
- Buzzwords: "passionate", "driven", "innovative", "synergy"

## When in doubt
- If information is missing or unclear, ask before writing. Never invent details about Pamela's process, experience, or work.

## Tone checks
- If it sounds like a LinkedIn headline, rewrite it.
- If it could apply to any designer, it's too generic — make it specific to Pamela.
- Credentials go in lists, not in prose declarations.

## Structure
- Each paragraph should have one job. Don't mix career history with teaching with personal values.
- End sections on the strongest line, not an afterthought.
